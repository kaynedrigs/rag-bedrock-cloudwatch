# Filipino Halal Recipes — Basic Collection

This is a compact collection of basic Filipino recipes adapted to be halal-friendly (no pork, no alcohol). Measurements are flexible — adjust salt and seasoning to taste. Each recipe follows the format you provided: Description, Ingredients, Procedure.

# Chicken Adobo

## Description

Chicken Adobo is the classic Filipino stewed chicken in a tangy-salty soy-vinegar sauce. This halal version uses chicken and omits any non-halal ingredients.

## Ingredients

* 1 kg chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks recommended)
* 1/2 cup soy sauce (use halal-certified if available)
* 1/2 cup cane vinegar or white vinegar
* 1/2 cup water
* 6 cloves garlic, crushed
* 2 bay leaves
* 1 tsp whole peppercorns
* 2 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Combine soy sauce, vinegar, water, crushed garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns in a bowl. Add chicken and marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the fridge).
2. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Remove chicken from marinade (reserve marinade) and brown the chicken lightly on all sides.
3. Pour the reserved marinade into the pan, add water if needed to nearly cover the chicken, and bring to a boil.
4. Lower heat and simmer, covered, for 25–30 minutes until chicken is cooked through and tender.
5. Remove lid and cook uncovered for another 5–10 minutes to reduce the sauce. Stir in brown sugar if you like a hint of sweetness.
6. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve with steamed rice.

# Tinolang Manok (Chicken Ginger Soup)

## Description

Tinola is a comforting Filipino chicken soup flavored with ginger, green papaya (or chayote), and malunggay (moringa) or spinach.

## Ingredients

* 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
* 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced into strips
* 1 medium onion, sliced
* 3 cloves garlic, crushed
* 2 cups diced green papaya or chayote
* 5 cups water or chicken broth (homemade or store-bought halal)
* 2 cups malunggay (moringa) leaves or spinach
* 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis) or salt to taste
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil

## Procedure

1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
2. Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
3. Pour in water or broth. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
4. Add green papaya or chayote and simmer another 8–10 minutes until tender.
5. Season with fish sauce or salt. Stir in malunggay leaves or spinach just before turning off the heat.
6. Serve hot with rice.

# Sinigang na Bangus (Sour Soup with Milkfish)

## Description

Sinigang is a tangy tamarind-based soup. This version uses bangus (milkfish) for a bright, savory seafood flavor.

## Ingredients

* 1 whole bangus (milkfish), cleaned and butterflied (about 600–800 g)
* 1 medium onion, quartered
* 2 tomatoes, quartered
* 1 radish (labanos), sliced
* 1 cup daikon or taro (optional), cubed
* 2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or pechay
* 1–2 packs tamarind soup base (sinigang mix) or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind extract
* 6 cups water
* 1–2 green chilies (siling haba), optional
* Fish sauce or salt to taste

## Procedure

1. In a pot, bring water to a boil with onion and tomatoes. Simmer for 5 minutes.
2. Add radish and any root vegetables. Cook until just tender.
3. Add the fish carefully and simmer for 6–8 minutes until cooked through.
4. Stir in tamarind mix (or fresh tamarind) and adjust sourness.
5. Add kangkong or pechay and green chilies. Season with fish sauce or salt.
6. Serve immediately with steamed rice.

# Chicken Inasal (Grilled Chicken)

## Description

Chicken Inasal is a Visayan-style grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of citrus, garlic, and annatto. Serve with steamed rice and atchara (pickled papaya).

## Ingredients

* 1 whole chicken, cut into serving parts (or 1 kg legs/thighs)
* 1/2 cup calamansi juice or lemon juice
* 1/4 cup soy sauce
* 6 cloves garlic, minced
* 2 tbsp brown sugar
* 1 tbsp annatto oil or 1 tsp annatto powder dissolved in 2 tbsp oil (for color)
* 1 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
* Bamboo skewers (optional)

## Procedure

1. Mix calamansi juice, soy sauce, minced garlic, brown sugar, annatto oil, salt, and pepper to make the marinade.
2. Marinate chicken for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
3. Grill chicken over medium heat, basting occasionally with the remaining marinade or oil, until cooked and slightly charred (about 20–30 minutes), turning to cook evenly.
4. Serve hot with rice and atchara.

# Pancit Canton (Stir-Fried Noodles with Chicken & Shrimp)

## Description

Pancit Canton is a stir-fried egg noodle dish with vegetables, chicken, and shrimp — a popular everyday and celebration dish.

## Ingredients

* 300 g pancit canton noodles (egg noodles)
* 200 g boneless chicken breast, thinly sliced
* 150 g shrimp, peeled and deveined
* 2 cups napa cabbage, sliced
* 1 cup carrots, julienned
* 1 cup snow peas or green beans
* 4 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 medium onion, sliced
* 3 cups chicken broth
* 3 tbsp soy sauce
* 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Heat oil in a large wok or pan. Sauté garlic and onion until translucent.
2. Add chicken slices and cook until no longer pink. Add shrimp and cook briefly until pink. Remove and set aside.
3. Pour in chicken broth, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Bring to a simmer.
4. Add carrots, cabbage, and green beans; cook for 2–3 minutes.
5. Add pancit canton noodles and toss to coat and soften. Add more broth if needed.
6. Return chicken and shrimp to the pan, stir to combine and heat through.
7. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve with calamansi wedges.

# Beef Caldereta (Tomato-Based Beef Stew)

## Description

Beef Caldereta is a hearty Filipino beef stew with tomatoes, potatoes, carrots, and sometimes liver spread. For a halal-friendly version, omit any non-halal additives and use halal beef.

## Ingredients

* 1 kg beef chuck, cubed
* 2 medium onions, chopped
* 4 cloves garlic, minced
* 3 medium potatoes, cubed
* 2 medium carrots, sliced
* 1 red bell pepper, sliced
* 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes or 2 cups fresh tomato sauce
* 1 cup beef broth
* 2 tbsp soy sauce
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* 2 tbsp liver spread (optional) — ensure halal source or omit
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Heat oil in a heavy pot. Brown beef cubes in batches and set aside.
2. Sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Return beef to the pot.
3. Add crushed tomatoes, beef broth, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 1–1.5 hours until beef is tender.
4. Add potatoes and carrots and simmer until softened.
5. Stir in bell pepper and liver spread (if using). Simmer 5–10 minutes to meld flavors.
6. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve with rice.

# Tortang Talong (Eggplant Omelette)

## Description

Tortang Talong is a simple and popular Filipino eggplant omelette — great as a main or side dish.

## Ingredients

* 3 large eggplants
* 3 large eggs
* 1 small onion, finely chopped
* 1 small tomato, finely chopped (optional)
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Grill or roast eggplants until the skin chars and flesh softens. Peel off skin and flatten the flesh with a fork, keeping the stem intact if possible.
2. Mix eggs, chopped onion, tomato, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
3. Dip each flattened eggplant into the egg mixture, coating both sides.
4. Heat oil in a pan and fry each eggplant until golden brown on both sides.
5. Serve hot with rice and soy-vinegar dipping sauce.

# Ginataang Kalabasa at Sitaw (Squash & String Beans in Coconut Milk)

## Description

A creamy, mildly sweet dish of squash and string beans cooked in coconut milk — vegetarian-friendly and naturally halal.

## Ingredients

* 1 medium kalabasa (squash), peeled and cubed (about 4 cups)
* 2 cups sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
* 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
* 1 cup water or vegetable broth
* 1 medium onion, chopped
* 3 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced (optional)
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in oil until fragrant.
2. Add squash and stir for 2 minutes.
3. Pour in water or broth and simmer until squash is almost tender.
4. Add coconut milk and string beans. Simmer until vegetables are tender and sauce has thickened.
5. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with rice.

# Laing (Taro Leaves in Coconut Milk) — Vegetarian Version

## Description

Laing is a Bicolano dish of dried taro leaves simmered in coconut milk and spices. This halal-friendly vegetarian version omits any bagoong or shrimp paste; use salted fish or soy sauce if you want umami.

## Ingredients

* 200 g dried taro leaves (or fresh, but dried is traditional) — handle with care; do not ingest raw leaves
* 2 cups coconut milk
* 1 cup coconut cream
* 1 medium onion, chopped
* 4 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 thumb-sized ginger, minced
* 2–3 long chilies (siling haba), sliced (optional)
* 2 tbsp vegetable oil
* Salt or soy sauce to taste

## Procedure

1. In a pan, sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in oil until fragrant.
2. Add coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
3. Add dried taro leaves in batches, pressing them down to submerge. Simmer gently — do not boil vigorously to avoid irritation from raw leaves.
4. Add chilies and simmer until leaves are fully tender and sauce reduces.
5. Stir in coconut cream and simmer 3–5 more minutes. Season with salt or soy sauce.
6. Serve with rice.

# Escabeche na Isda (Sweet & Sour Fried Fish)

## Description

Escabeche is a fillet of fried fish topped with a bright sweet-and-sour sauce with bell peppers and onions.

## Ingredients

* 1 whole bangus (milkfish) butterflied or 4 fish fillets (tilapia, sea bass)
* 1/2 cup all-purpose flour for dusting
* Oil for frying
* 1 medium onion, sliced
* 1 carrot, julienned
* 1 red bell pepper, sliced
* 1/2 cup white vinegar
* 1/4 cup sugar
* 1 cup water or fish broth
* 2 tbsp soy sauce
* 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)
* Salt and pepper to taste

## Procedure

1. Pat fish dry and dust lightly with flour. Deep-fry or pan-fry until golden and set aside on a rack.
2. In a saucepan, sauté onion until translucent. Add carrot and bell pepper and cook briefly.
3. Add vinegar, sugar, water, and soy sauce. Bring to a simmer.
4. Stir in cornstarch slurry to thicken. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Pour sauce over the fried fish and serve with rice.

---

*Notes & Tips*

* Adjust spice and sourness to taste (calamansi/lemon, tamarind, or sinigang mix).
* When using fish sauce (patis), it is generally acceptable in halal diets; if in doubt, substitute with soy sauce or salt.
* For any canned or processed ingredient, check for halal certification if required.
